How Calculations Work

Overview

This calculator determines the monetary value lost per hour when mining operations experience downtime. It considers the ore processing rate, metal grade, recovery efficiency, and current market prices.

Gold & Silver Calculation

For precious metals measured in grams per tonne (g/t):

Step 1: Calculate grams of metal
Tonnes × Grade (g/t) = Grams of Metal
Step 2: Apply recovery rate
Grams × (Recovery % ÷ 100) = Recovered Grams
Step 3: Convert to troy ounces
Recovered Grams ÷ 31.1035 = Troy Ounces
Step 4: Calculate value
Troy Ounces × Price per Ounce = Value Lost
Example (Gold):
• 125 tonnes/hour × 2 g/t = 250 grams
• 250 grams × 90% recovery = 225 grams
• 225 ÷ 31.1035 = 7.23 troy oz
• 7.23 oz × $2,000 = $14,466/hour lost

Copper Calculation

For base metals measured in percentage (%):

Step 1: Calculate kilograms of copper
Tonnes × Grade (%) × 10 = Kilograms of Copper
Note: 1% grade = 10 kg per tonne
Step 2: Apply recovery rate
Kilograms × (Recovery % ÷ 100) = Recovered Kg
Step 3: Convert to pounds
Recovered Kg × 2.20462 = Pounds
Step 4: Calculate value
Pounds × Price per Pound = Value Lost
Example (Copper):
• 125 tonnes/hour × 2% × 10 = 2,500 kg
• 2,500 kg × 90% recovery = 2,250 kg
• 2,250 × 2.20462 = 4,960 lbs
• 4,960 lbs × $4.50 = $22,320/hour lost

Key Constants

  • 31.1035 grams = 1 troy ounce (precious metals standard)
  • 2.20462 pounds = 1 kilogram
  • 1% copper grade = 10 kg per tonne

Input Parameters

  • Metal Price: Current market price (USD/oz for gold/silver, USD/lb for copper)
  • Tonnes per Hour: Ore processing rate during normal operations
  • Grade: Metal content concentration (g/t for gold/silver, % for copper)
  • Recovery Rate: Percentage of metal successfully extracted (typically 85-95%)
